Angle Rules · Unit 4
VERTICAL ANGLES — formed by two intersecting lines, across from each other. They are always EQUAL.
LINEAR PAIR — two angles that share a side and form a straight line. They are SUPPLEMENTARY (sum to 180°).
COMPLEMENTARY — two angles that sum to 90°.
SUPPLEMENTARY — two angles that sum to 180°.

When two parallel lines are cut by a transversal:

CORRESPONDING ANGLES — same position at each intersection. EQUAL.
ALTERNATE INTERIOR — between the lines, opposite sides of the transversal. EQUAL.
ALTERNATE EXTERIOR — outside the lines, opposite sides of the transversal. EQUAL.
SAME-SIDE INTERIOR (CO-INTERIOR) — between the lines, same side of the transversal. SUPPLEMENTARY (sum to 180°).
TRIANGLE ANGLE SUM — the three interior angles of ANY triangle sum to 180°.
EXTERIOR ANGLE THEOREM — an exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two REMOTE (non-adjacent) interior angles.
Common setup: exterior = remote₁ + remote₂.  If exterior = 120° and one remote = 50°, the other is 70°.
