Exponent Solitaire

How to Play

Your goal: Move all 28 cards onto the four foundation piles at the top. Each foundation is labeled with a base (x, y, m, or n). Build each one up from exponent 1 all the way to 7.

Every Card Shows an Unsimplified Expression

Like x2 · x3 or (2y3)2. Figure out what it simplifies to before placing it.

Where You Can Move a Card

  • To a foundation: The card must simplify to that foundation's base with the next exponent needed. If the x-pile shows x3, you need a card that simplifies to x4.
  • To a tableau column: Build down by exponent, any base. A card simplifying to y5 can go on any card simplifying to exponent 6.
  • To an empty column: Any card works.
  • Moving a stack: Select a card partway down a column to move it and everything below it — but only if those cards form a valid descending sequence.

The Stock Pile

Click the face-down stock pile to flip a card to the waste. The top waste card is playable. When stock empties, click the empty spot to recycle the waste.

How to Move

Click a card to select it (it glows gold). Then click where you want to move it. Click it again to deselect.

Stuck?

Click Show Simplified for training-wheels mode — each card reveals what it simplifies to. Hit New Deal any time for a fresh game. Press Esc to deselect or close dialogs.

Difficulty Levels

  • Easy: Product, quotient, and power-of-a-power rules.
  • Medium: Adds zero exponents and negative exponents inside expressions.
  • Hard: Adds monomials with coefficients — like 3x2 · 4x3 = 12x5.

🏆 You Win!

You simplified every expression and filled all four foundations.
