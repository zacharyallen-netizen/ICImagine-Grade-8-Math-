① Converting Between Decimals and Fractions

Key Idea: Every decimal can be written as a fraction, and every fraction can be written as a decimal. Some decimals terminate (end), while others repeat (go on forever in a pattern).

Part A: Terminating Decimals → Fractions

  1. Read the decimal using place value (tenths, hundredths, thousandths…).
  2. Write the digits as the numerator over the place value as the denominator.
  3. Simplify by dividing numerator and denominator by the GCF.
Example 1: Convert 0.75 to a fraction.
0.75 → "seventy-five hundredths" → 75100
GCF(75, 100) = 25
75 ÷ 25 ⁄ 100 ÷ 25 = 34
Example 2: Convert 0.125 to a fraction.
0.125 → "one hundred twenty-five thousandths" → 1251000
GCF(125, 1000) = 125
125 ÷ 125 ⁄ 1000 ÷ 125 = 18

Part B: Fractions → Decimals (Long Division)

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator using long division.
  2. The result will either terminate (end) or repeat (show a pattern).
Example 3: Convert 38 to a decimal.
3 ÷ 8 — use long division:
0.375 ┌────── 8 │ 3.000 │ 2 4 ↓ 8 × 3 = 24 │ ─── │ 60 │ 56 ↓ 8 × 7 = 56 │ ─── │ 40 │ 40 8 × 5 = 40 │ ── │ 0 ← no remainder!
38 = 0.375 — this is a terminating decimal.
Example 4: Convert 56 to a decimal.
5 ÷ 6 — use long division:
0.833... ┌────── 6 │ 5.000 │ 4 8 ↓ 6 × 8 = 48 │ ─── │ 20 │ 18 ↓ 6 × 3 = 18 │ ─── │ 20 │ 18 6 × 3 = 18 │ ── │ 2 ← same remainder — it repeats!
The 3 repeats forever. We write this with bar notation:
56 = 0.83   (the bar over the 3 means it repeats forever)

Part C: Repeating Decimals → Fractions (Quick Pattern)

There's a powerful shortcut for converting repeating decimals to fractions:

⚡ The Quick Pattern:

One digit repeats? Put it over 9.

0.7  =  79      0.4  =  49

Two digits repeat? Put them over 99.

0.21  =  2199  =  733      0.36  =  3699  =  411

Three digits repeat? Put them over 999.

0.125  =  125999

⚠ Always simplify the resulting fraction!

Example 5: Convert 0.63 to a fraction.
Two digits repeat → put over 99: 6399
GCF(63, 99) = 9
63 ÷ 9 ⁄ 99 ÷ 9 = 711
Example 6: Convert 0.54 to a fraction.
Two digits repeat → put over 99: 5499
GCF(54, 99) = 9
54 ÷ 9 ⁄ 99 ÷ 9 = 611

② Estimating Non-Perfect Square Roots

Key Idea: Estimate square roots by finding the two consecutive perfect squares the target falls between, then refining with a fraction.

Reference: Perfect Squares You Should Know

The Estimation Algorithm

  1. Find the two perfect squares the target falls between.
  2. The lesser square root is the integer part of your estimate.
  3. Build a fraction:
target number − lesser perfect square
greater perfect square − lesser perfect square
  1. Convert that fraction to a decimal.
  2. Add the decimal to the lesser square root for your estimate.
Example 1: Estimate √18
Step 1: 16 < 18 < 25
Step 2: √16 = 4 and √25 = 5 → Integer part = 4
Step 3: 18 − 1625 − 16 = 29
Step 4: 2 ÷ 9 ≈ 0.222
Step 5: 4 + 0.222 = √18 ≈ 4.222
Check: 4.222² ≈ 17.83 ✓
Example 2: Estimate √55
Step 1: 49 < 55 < 64
Step 2: √49 = 7 and √64 = 8 → Integer part = 7
Step 3: 55 − 4964 − 49 = 615 = 25
Step 4: 2 ÷ 5 = 0.4
Step 5: 7 + 0.4 = √55 ≈ 7.4
Check: 7.4² = 54.76 ✓

③ Ordering Mixed Numbers

Key Idea: Convert everything to the same form (decimals or common denominator) to compare directly.

Method 1: Convert Everything to Decimals

  1. Convert each mixed number / fraction to a decimal.
  2. Compare the decimals.
  3. Write the original numbers in order.
Example 1: Order least to greatest:  234,   2.6,   258
234 = 2.75     2.6 = 2.6     258 = 2.625
2.6 < 2.625 < 2.75
Answer: 2.6,   2 58,   2 34

Method 2: Find a Common Denominator

  1. Compare whole parts first — different wholes determine order immediately.
  2. For same whole parts, find the LCD of the fraction parts.
  3. Compare numerators.
Example 2: Order least to greatest:  313,   325,   314
Same whole part (3). LCD(3, 5, 4) = 60
13 = 2060    25 = 2460    14 = 1560
Answer: 3 14,   3 13,   3 25

Dealing with Negatives

Remember: For two negative numbers, the one with the larger absolute value is less. Farther left on the number line = smaller.

Example 3: Order:  −112,   34,   −0.8,   113
−1 12 = −1.5    34 = 0.75    −0.8    1 13 ≈ 1.333
−1.5 < −0.8 < 0.75 < 1.333
Answer: −1 12,   −0.8,   34,   1 13
