📖 Math Vocabulary Glossary

U1
Rational Number
A number that CAN be written as a fraction a/b where b≠0. Includes integers, terminating decimals, and repeating decimals.
📌 3/4, −7, 0.5, 0.333...
💡 Every fraction and every integer is rational.
U1
Irrational Number
A number that CANNOT be written as a fraction. Its decimal goes on forever without repeating.
📌 π, √2, √10, 0.10100100010...
💡 If the square root is not a perfect square, it's irrational.
U1
Perfect Square
A number that is the result of an integer multiplied by itself.
📌 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, 121, 144
💡 √49 = 7 because 7 × 7 = 49
U2
Exponent
A small number that tells you how many times to multiply the base by itself.
📌 x⁵ = x·x·x·x·x (5 times)
💡 In 2³, the 2 is the base and 3 is the exponent.
U2
Scientific Notation
A way to write very large or very small numbers: a × 10ⁿ where 1 ≤ a < 10.
📌 4.5 × 10⁷ = 45,000,000
💡 Positive exponent = big number. Negative = small number.
U2
Monomial
A number, variable, or product of numbers and variables with whole number exponents.
📌 5x², −3ab, 7, x³y
💡 Multiply monomials: multiply coefficients, add exponents.
U3
Variable
A letter that represents an unknown value.
📌 x, y, n, a
💡 In 3x + 5 = 14, x is the variable (x = 3).
U3
Inverse Operations
Operations that undo each other. Used to solve equations.
📌 + ↔ − and × ↔ ÷
💡 To solve 3x + 5 = 14: subtract 5, then divide by 3.
U3
Inequality
A math sentence comparing two expressions using <, >, ≤, or ≥.
📌 x > 3, 2y + 1 ≤ 9
💡 FLIP the sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative!
U3
No Solution
An equation where the variables cancel and you get a FALSE statement.
📌 3 = 7 or 0 = 5
💡 Example: 2x + 1 = 2x + 4 simplifies to 1 = 4 (false).
U3
Infinite Solutions
An equation where the variables cancel and you get a TRUE statement.
📌 5 = 5 or 0 = 0
💡 Example: 3(x+2) = 3x+6 simplifies to 6 = 6 (always true).
U4
Supplementary Angles
Two angles that add up to 180°.
📌 120° + 60° = 180°
💡 Linear pairs are always supplementary.
U4
Complementary Angles
Two angles that add up to 90°.
📌 55° + 35° = 90°
💡 Think: C for Corner (90°), S for Straight (180°).
U4
Vertical Angles
Opposite angles formed by two intersecting lines. Always EQUAL.
📌 If one is 72°, the other is 72°
💡 They form an X shape — the angles across from each other match.
U4
Transversal
A line that crosses two or more other lines, creating angle pairs.
📌 Creates 8 angles at two intersections
💡 With parallel lines: corresponding, alternate interior/exterior, co-interior.
U5
Hypotenuse
The LONGEST side of a right triangle, across from the right angle.
📌 Always labeled c in a² + b² = c²
💡 If asked to find the hypotenuse, you ADD the squares of the legs.
U5
Leg
One of the two shorter sides of a right triangle that form the right angle.
📌 Labeled a and b
💡 If asked to find a leg, you SUBTRACT the squares.
U5
Pythagorean Triple
A set of three whole numbers that satisfy a² + b² = c².
📌 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, 7-24-25
💡 Multiples also work: 6-8-10, 9-12-15, etc.
U6
Function
A rule where each input (x) has exactly ONE output (y).
📌 f(x) = 2x + 3
💡 Passes the vertical line test — every x has only one y.
U6
Slope
The steepness of a line. Rate of change. Rise over run.
📌 m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)
💡 Positive=up, Negative=down, Zero=flat, Undefined=vertical.
U6
Y-intercept
The point where a line crosses the y-axis (where x = 0).
📌 In y = 3x + 5, the y-intercept is 5
💡 It's the b in y = mx + b. The starting value.
U6
System of Equations
Two equations with the same variables. The solution is where they intersect.
📌 y = 2x + 1 and y = −x + 7
💡 Solution: the (x,y) point that works in BOTH equations.
U7
Scatterplot
A graph using dots to show the relationship between two sets of data.
📌 Each dot = one data pair (x,y)
💡 Look for patterns: positive, negative, or no correlation.
U7
Correlation
The direction and strength of the relationship in a scatterplot.
📌 Positive, Negative, or None
💡 Strong = points close to a line. Weak = more spread out.
U7
Line of Best Fit
A straight line that best represents the trend in a scatterplot.
📌 Approx. equal points above and below
💡 Use the equation to PREDICT values not in the data.
U7
Outlier
A data point far away from the pattern of the other data.
📌 A dot clearly separated from the cluster
💡 Can distort the line of best fit — identify before drawing.
U7
Two-Way Table
A table showing the frequency of two categories of data.
📌 Rows = one category, Columns = another
💡 Joint frequency = one cell. Marginal = row/column total.
U8
Translation
A transformation that SLIDES a figure without changing size or shape.
📌 (x,y) → (x+a, y+b)
💡 Every point moves the same distance in the same direction.
U8
Reflection
A transformation that FLIPS a figure over a line (mirror image).
📌 x-axis: (x,y)→(x,−y)
💡 The image is the same distance from the line as the original.
U8
Rotation
A transformation that TURNS a figure around a point.
📌 90° CCW: (x,y)→(−y,x)
💡 Most rotations are about the origin. Know 90°, 180°, 270° rules.
U8
Dilation
A transformation that changes the SIZE of a figure by scale factor k.
📌 (x,y) → (kx, ky)
💡 k>1: enlarge. 0
U8
Congruent
Same size AND shape. Produced by rigid motions.
📌 ≅ symbol
💡 Translations, reflections, rotations all preserve congruence.
U8
Similar
Same shape, proportional sides, equal angles. Produced by dilations.
📌 ~ symbol
💡 All congruent figures are similar, but not vice versa.
VOL
Cylinder
3D figure with two circular bases. V = πr²h.
📌 Like a can
💡 Base area (circle) × height.
VOL
Cone
3D figure with one circular base tapering to a point. V = ⅓πr²h.
📌 Like an ice cream cone
💡 3 cones fill 1 cylinder with same r and h.
VOL
Sphere
Perfectly round 3D figure. V = ⁴⁄₃πr³.
📌 Like a basketball
💡 Only needs radius. Uses r CUBED not squared!
VOL
Scale Factor
The number multiplied by each side length in a dilation.
📌 k = 2 means double, k = 0.5 means half
💡 k > 1 enlarges, 0 < k < 1 reduces.
