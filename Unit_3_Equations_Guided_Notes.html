2 Two-Step Equations

📌 TWO STEPS: Step 1 — Undo addition/subtraction | Step 2 — Undo multiplication/division

Example 1: −2x + 4 = 8

Step 1 — Undo addition (subtract 4): −2x + 4 − 4 = 8 − 4 → −2x = click to reveal 4
✓ Step 2 — Undo multiplication (÷ −2): x = click to reveal −2

Example 2: w/2 + 6 = 7

✓ Step 2 — Undo division (× 2): w = click to reveal 2

3 Distributive Property

📌 THE DISTRIBUTIVE PROPERTY: Any number (or variable) outside of the parentheses is multiplied by everything inside the parentheses. Pay special attention to positive/negative signs!

Example: 2(2x + 3) = −6

1. Distribute: 2(2x) + 2(3) = click to reveal 4x + 6 = −6
2. Undo addition (subtract 6): 4x = click to reveal −12
✓ 3. Undo multiplication (÷ 4): x = click to reveal −3
ERROR ANALYSIS A student expands −3(x − 4) as −3x − 12. What went wrong?
A negative times a negative is positive: −3 · (−4) = +12. Correct: −3x + 12. Distribute the sign to every term inside.

4 Variables on Both Sides

📌 STRATEGY: Move variables from the RIGHT to the LEFT using inverse operations, then solve like a two-step equation.

Example: 5x + 3 = 2x + 12

Move variables (subtract 2x): 3x + 3 = click to reveal 12
Undo addition (subtract 3): 3x = click to reveal 9
✓ Undo multiplication (÷ 3): x = click to reveal 3

5 Multi-Step Equations — The 5-Step Process

The 5 steps: 1. Distribute → 2. Combine Like Terms → 3. Move Variables → 4. Inverse for + or − → 5. Inverse for × or ÷

Example: −2x + 1 = 8(x + 2) − 5x

1. Distribute (8·x and 8·2): −2x + 1 = click to reveal 8x + 16 − 5x
2. Combine like terms (8x − 5x): −2x + 1 = click to reveal 3x + 16
3. Move variables (+2x): 1 = click to reveal 5x + 16
4. Undo addition (−16): −15 = click to reveal 5x
✓ 5. Undo multiplication (÷5): x = click to reveal −3

SHORT ANSWER Problem 1 (Equation) — Movie Theater: A movie theater charges a $6 entry fee plus $4 per ticket. If the total cost was $30, how many tickets were purchased?
Let x = number of tickets → 6 + 4x = 30. Solve: 4x = 24 → x = 6 → 6 tickets were purchased.
SHORT ANSWER Problem 2 (Inequality) — Notebooks: A student wants to spend no more than $50 on notebooks that cost $6 each. How many notebooks can the student buy?
Let x = number of notebooks → 6x ≤ 50 ("no more than" = ≤). Solve: x ≤ 8.33 → at most 8 notebooks.

D Word Problems

Strategy: Define a variable → Write an equation or inequality → Solve → Check if your answer makes sense!
1. A phone plan costs $35/month plus $0.10 per text. Last month's bill was $52. How many texts were sent?
Let t = texts → 35 + 0.10t = 52. Solve: 0.10t = 17 → t = 170 texts.
2. Sam has $240 and saves $30/week. Jake has $420 and spends $10/week. When will they have the same amount?
Let w = weeks → 240 + 30w = 420 − 10w. Solve: 40w = 180 → w = 4.5 weeks.
3. A rectangle's perimeter is 56 cm. The length is 4 cm more than the width. Find the width.
Let w = width → 2(w + 4) + 2w = 56. Solve: 4w + 8 = 56 → w = 12 cm.
4. Movie tickets cost $9 each. You have at most $50 to spend. What is the maximum number of tickets?
Let t = tickets → 9t ≤ 50 ("at most" = ≤). Solve: t ≤ 5.56 → max 5 tickets (must be a whole number).
5. A car rental costs $45/day plus $0.25/mile. Your budget is $120 for one day. How many miles can you drive?
Let m = miles → 45 + 0.25m ≤ 120. Solve: 0.25m ≤ 75 → m ≤ 300 miles.
6. Three consecutive even integers sum to 78. Find the smallest integer.
Let n = smallest → n + (n+2) + (n+4) = 78. Solve: 3n + 6 = 78 → n = 24.
