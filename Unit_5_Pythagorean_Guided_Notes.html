1 The Pythagorean Theorem

📌 THE THEOREM: In a RIGHT triangle, the sum of the squares of the two LEGS equals the square of the HYPOTENUSE.
a² + b² = c²
a, b = legs (shorter sides)  |  c = hypotenuse (longest side, across from the right angle)
⚠️ IMPORTANT: The Pythagorean Theorem ONLY works on RIGHT triangles (triangles with a 90° angle)!

2 Finding the Hypotenuse

📌 WHEN TO USE: When you know BOTH LEGS and need to find the HYPOTENUSE. Formula: c² = a² + b² → c = √(a² + b²)

Example: Legs are 6 and 8. Find the hypotenuse.

Step 1 — Write formula: a² + b² = c²
Step 2 — Substitute: 6² + 8² = c²
Step 3 — Square: 36 + 64 = c²
Step 4 — Add: 100 = c²
✓ Step 5 — Square root: c = √100 = click to reveal 10

3 Finding a Missing Leg

📌 WHEN TO USE: When you know ONE LEG and the HYPOTENUSE. Formula: a² = c² − b² → a = √(c² − b²)
💡 Finding a LEG = SUBTRACT from the hypotenuse squared. Finding the HYPOTENUSE = ADD the two legs squared.

Example: One leg is 5, hypotenuse is 13. Find the other leg.

Step 1 — Write formula: a² + b² = c²
Step 2 — Substitute: 5² + b² = 13²
Step 3 — Square: 25 + b² = 169
Step 4 — Subtract 25 (finding a LEG = SUBTRACT): b² = click to reveal 144
✓ Step 5 — Square root: b = √144 = click to reveal 12
ERROR ANALYSIS A student finds a missing leg when c = 13, b = 5 by writing a² = 13² + 5² = 194. What's wrong?
To find a leg you SUBTRACT: a² = c² − b² = 169 − 25 = 144 → a = 12. Adding is only for the hypotenuse.

4 Converse of the Pythagorean Theorem

📌 THE CONVERSE: Given three side lengths a ≤ b ≤ c, test: does a² + b² = c²? If YES → right triangle. If NO → not a right triangle. Always test the two SMALLER sides against the LARGEST.
MC Sides 3, 4, 5 — right triangle?
✓ 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25; 5² = 25; 25 = 25 → YES, right triangle.
MC Sides 2, 3, 4 — right triangle?
✓ 2² + 3² = 4 + 9 = 13; 4² = 16; 13 ≠ 16 → NO, not a right triangle.
MC Sides 5, 12, 13 — right triangle?
✓ 5² + 12² = 25 + 144 = 169; 13² = 169; 169 = 169 → YES, right triangle.
MC Sides 6, 7, 10 — right triangle?
✓ 6² + 7² = 36 + 49 = 85; 10² = 100; 85 ≠ 100 → NO, not a right triangle.

6 Distance on the Coordinate Plane

📌 HOW IT WORKS: Connect the two points to form a right triangle. The horizontal distance is ∆x = |x₂ − x₁| and the vertical distance is ∆y = |y₂ − y₁|. Then use a² + b² = c² where a = ∆x, b = ∆y, and c = distance.
💡 NO DISTANCE FORMULA NEEDED! You already know how to do this — just use the Pythagorean Theorem!

Example: Distance from (0, −2) to (6, 2)

Right triangle on the grid
∆x: |6 − 0| = click to reveal 6
∆y: |2 − (−2)| = click to reveal 4
Setup: ∆x² + ∆y² = d² → click to reveal 36 + 16 = 52
✓ Answer: d = √52 ≈ click to reveal 7.21
⚠️ WATCH YOUR SIGNS: When subtracting negative coordinates, remember: subtracting a negative means ADDING! Example: 5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8, NOT 5 − 3 = 2.

C Word Problems

Strategy: Draw a picture! Label the sides. Decide: finding hypotenuse (ADD squares) or a leg (SUBTRACT squares)?
1. A 13-foot ladder leans against a wall. The base is 5 feet from the wall. How high does it reach?
h² + 5² = 13² → h² = 169 − 25 = 144 → h = 12 ft.
2. A rectangular TV is 36 inches wide and 15 inches tall. What is the diagonal length?
d² = 36² + 15² = 1296 + 225 = 1521 → d = 39 in.
3. A baseball diamond is a square with 90-ft sides. Distance from home to second base?
d² = 90² + 90² = 16200 → d ≈ 127.3 ft.
4. A kite string is 100 m long. The kite is directly above a point 60 m away. How high is the kite?
h² + 60² = 100² → h² = 10000 − 3600 = 6400 → h = 80 m.
5. A support beam covers 9 ft horizontal and 12 ft vertical. How long is the beam?
c² = 9² + 12² = 81 + 144 = 225 → c = 15 ft.
6. A pool is 24 m long and 10 m wide. A swimmer swims diagonally. How far?
d² = 24² + 10² = 576 + 100 = 676 → d = 26 m.
