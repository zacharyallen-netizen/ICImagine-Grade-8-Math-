6.4 Linear Functions in Real-World Context

🔑 Writing y = mx + b from a Word Problem

m (slope) = the rate of change. Look for: "per," "each," "every," "for each additional"

b (y-intercept) = the starting value. Look for: "initial," "flat fee," "base," "one-time," "deposit"

ScenarioLinear?Why?
A car travels at 60 mph✓Constant rate — steady speed
Babysitter charges $25 + $15/hr✓Fixed fee + constant hourly rate
A plant doubles in height each week✗Doubling = exponential, not constant
Example: Plumber charges $80 after 1 hour, $140 after 3 hours.

Step 1: Points → (1, 80) and (3, 140)

Step 2: Slope → m = (140 − 80) ÷ (3 − 1) = 60 ÷ 2 = 30

Step 3: Find b → 80 = 30(1) + b → b = 50

Equation: y = 30x + 50 — $30/hour with a $50 service call fee.

📝 Interpreting m and b in Context

Slope: "For every 1 more [x-unit], the [y-quantity] changes by [m]."

Y-intercept: "When [x] = 0, [y] is [b]."

Example: y = −1500x + 24000 → The car loses $1,500/year and was worth $24,000 when new.

✎ Practice: Real World

1 A taxi charges $3 flat + $2.50/mile. Write the equation and find the cost for 7 miles.
m = 2.50 (per mile), b = 3 (flat fee). For 7 miles: 2.50(7) + 3.
2 y = −1500x + 24000 models a car's value. What is the car worth after 5 years?

6.7 Systems of Equations

🔑 What is a System?

Two equations, two unknowns. The solution is the (x, y) point that makes both equations true at the same time.

Three Possible Outcomes

One Solution

Lines cross.
Different slopes.

No Solution

Parallel — never meet.
Same slope, different b.

Infinite Solutions

Same line!
Same slope, same b.

⚡ Inspection — Quick Check
Compare SlopesResultExample
Different slopesONE solutiony = 3x + 2 and y = −x + 6
Same slope, different bNO solutiony = 3x + 2 and y = 3x − 5
Same slope, same bINFINITEy = 3x + 2 and 2y = 6x + 4

Method 1: Graphing

Graph both lines on the same coordinate plane. The intersection point is the solution.

Example: y = x − 8 and y = −2x + 1

Graph both. They intersect at (3, −5).

Check:   y = 3 − 8 = −5 ✓    y = −2(3) + 1 = −6 + 1 = −5 ✓

Method 2: Substitution

  1. Get one variable alone (y = ... or x = ...).
  2. Substitute that expression into the other equation.
  3. Solve for the remaining variable.
  4. Plug back in to find the other variable.
Example 1: y = 4x − 1 and y = 2x + 5
Both equal y, so set them equal:
4x − 1 = 2x + 5
−2x     −2x
2x − 1 = 5
2x = 6 → x = 3

Find y: y = 4(3) − 1 = y = 11
Solution: (3, 11)
Example 2: y = x + 9 and 3x + 8y = −5
Substitute (x + 9) for y:
3x + 8(x + 9) = −5
3x + 8x + 72 = −5
11x + 72 = −5
11x = −77 → x = −7

Find y: y = −7 + 9 = y = 2
Solution: (−7, 2)

✎ Practice: Systems

1 How many solutions?   y = 2x + 3  and  y = 2x − 1
Same slope (2), different y-intercepts (3 vs −1). Parallel!
2 How many solutions?   y = −x + 4  and  y = 3x − 8
3 Solve by substitution:   y = 3x + 1  and  y = x + 7.   Find the solution (x, y).
Set equal: 3x + 1 = x + 7. Subtract x: 2x + 1 = 7. Solve for x, then find y.
4 Solve by substitution:   y = −2x + 10  and  y = x + 1
Set equal: −2x + 10 = x + 1. Add 2x to both sides: 10 = 3x + 1.
5 Solve:   y = x + 3  and  2x + y = 12
Substitute (x + 3) for y: 2x + (x + 3) = 12 → 3x + 3 = 12.
6 Word Problem: Company A charges $20 + $0.30/mile. Company B charges $35 + $0.15/mile. At how many miles are they equal?
A: y = 0.30x + 20. B: y = 0.15x + 35. Set equal: 0.30x + 20 = 0.15x + 35.
