What is Slope?

Imagine you're walking up a hill. Some hills are steep, some are gentle. Slope is the mathematical way to measure that steepness.

Slope measures the rate of change — how much the y-value changes for every 1 unit change in x. It tells us two things:
1. How steep the line is
2. Which direction it goes (up or down, left to right)

We use the letter m to represent slope. In the equation y = mx + b:

m = the slope (steepness and direction)
b = the y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)
📝 Write this in your notes: "Slope = rate of change = rise over run = m"

Finding Slope from a Graph: Rise Over Run

To find slope from a graph, pick any two points on the line, then count:

Slope = rise (vertical change)run (horizontal change)

Rise = how far you move up or down (count vertical grid squares)
Run = how far you move left or right (count horizontal grid squares)

Example 1: Count the slope

Here's a line passing through (1, 2) and (4, 5).

The line with two points marked. Click Next to see the rise and run.

Example 1: Counting Rise and Run

Starting at (1, 2):

→ Count UP 3 squares (rise = 3)
→ Count RIGHT 3 squares to reach (4, 5) (run = 3)

Slope = riserun = 33 = 1

Example 2: A steeper line

Line through (1, 1) and (3, 5).

→ Rise = 4 (count up from 1 to 5)
→ Run = 2 (count right from 1 to 3)

Slope = 42 = 2

Example 3: Negative slope (going downhill)

Line through (1, 6) and (4, 3).

→ Rise = −3 (count DOWN from 6 to 3 — negative because you go down!)
→ Run = 3 (count right from 1 to 4)

Slope = −33 = −1
📝 If you count down, the rise is negative. That's what makes the slope negative!

The Slope Formula

When you have two points but no graph, use the slope formula:

m = y2 − y1x2 − x1
(x1, y1) is the first point
(x2, y2) is the second point
The numerator (top) is the rise. The denominator (bottom) is the run.

Example 1: Find the slope of the line through (2, 3) and (5, 9)

Step 1: Label your points

(x1, y1) = (2, 3)
(x2, y2) = (5, 9)

Example 1 (continued)

Step 2: Substitute into the formula

m = 9 − 35 − 2

Step 3: Simplify

m = 63 = 2
📝 The slope is 2, which means for every 1 unit you move right, you move up 2 units.

Example 2: (−1, 4) and (3, −2)

Step 1: Label your points

(x1, y1) = (−1, 4)
(x2, y2) = (3, −2)

Step 2: Substitute

m = −2 − 43 − (−1)

Example 2 (continued)

Step 3: Simplify carefully — watch the signs!

m = −64 = −32
Important: Always simplify your fraction! −6/4 reduces to −3/2.
The negative sign means the line goes downhill (left to right).
⚠️ Common mistake: 3 − (−1) = 3 + 1 = 4, NOT 3 − 1 = 2. Subtracting a negative = adding!

Example 3: (2, 5) and (6, 5)

m = 5 − 56 − 2 = 04 = 0
Zero in the numerator → slope = 0 → horizontal line!
Both y-values are the same, so there's no vertical change.

Example 4: (3, 1) and (3, 7)

m = 7 − 13 − 3 = 60 = undefined
Zero in the denominator → slope is undefined → vertical line!
Both x-values are the same. Division by zero is undefined.
