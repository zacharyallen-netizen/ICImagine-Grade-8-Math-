Describe correlation with: Direction (positive/negative/none) + Strength (strong/weak) + Form (linear/nonlinear) + Outliers
Question 1
Describe the correlation in this scatterplot.
Points cluster tightly along an upward trend — strong positive correlation. As practice hours increase, points scored increase. ✓
Question 2
What type of correlation does this scatterplot show?
Points fall steeply from upper-left to lower-right — strong negative correlation. More TV hours → lower test grades. ✓
Question 3
What does this scatterplot suggest?
No correlation — shoe size tells us nothing about science grade. The points are scattered without any pattern. ✓
Question 4
The red point in the scatterplot is an outlier. What is the best description of its position?
An outlier is a point that falls far from the general pattern. This red point is much lower than the trend line — it doesn't fit the overall negative correlation. ✓
Question 5
A student notices that cities with more hospitals also have higher crime rates. She concludes: "Hospitals cause crime." What is the flaw in her reasoning?
Correlation ≠ Causation. A third variable (city size) causes BOTH. Larger cities naturally have more hospitals AND more crime. This is a classic confounding variable. ✓
Question 6
Which statement describes a strong correlation vs. a weak correlation?
Strength is about how tightly the points hug the trend line. Strong = tight cluster; Weak = spread out. Has nothing to do with direction or number of points. ✓
Question 7
Which scatterplot type would best show a strong positive correlation?
Positive = upward direction. Strong = tight cluster. The answer is points that rise tightly from lower-left to upper-right. ✓
Question 8
Two students both plot "hours of sleep vs. reaction time." Student A finds r is strongly negative; Student B finds very little pattern. Which student's data best shows that more sleep improves reaction time?
A strong negative correlation here means: more sleep → LOWER reaction time (faster). Student A's data shows a clear pattern supporting the relationship. ✓
Question 9
A scatterplot for "age of car vs. resale value" would most likely show:
Older cars generally sell for less — as age increases, value decreases. This is a strong negative correlation. ✓
Question 10
What does the LINE OF BEST FIT on a scatterplot allow us to do?
The line of best fit models the trend so we can predict y for any given x. It does NOT prove causation, and doesn't connect all points. ✓

y = mx + b: m = slope (rate of change per unit of x) · b = y-intercept (starting value when x = 0)
Question 1
Using y = 8x + 52: what score is predicted for a student who studies 3 hours? (Find the green dot.)
y = 8(3) + 52 = 24 + 52 = 76 points ✓. Locate x=3 on the graph and read across to the green dot on the trend line.
Question 2
In the context of Study Hours vs. Test Score (y = 8x + 52), what does the slope of 8 mean?
Slope = rate of change: every 1 more hour studied → score predicted to be 8 points higher. ✓
Question 3
In the same equation y = 8x + 52, what does the y-intercept of 52 mean?
y-intercept = value of y when x = 0. When x = 0 hours: y = 8(0)+52 = 52 points predicted. ✓ (maybe prior knowledge/test-taking skill)
Question 4
Using y = −0.04x + 15 (x = miles driven, y = gallons remaining): how many gallons remain after 200 miles? (Find the green dot.)
y = −0.04(200) + 15 = −8 + 15 = 7 gallons ✓
Question 5
In the miles/gallons context, what does the slope of −0.04 mean?
Slope = −0.04: negative because fuel DECREASES with distance. Every mile driven uses about 0.04 gallons (= 25 mpg, since 1÷0.04=25). ✓
Question 6
A store sells lemonade. The line y = 3x − 60 predicts cups sold (y) based on temperature °F (x). Predict sales at 90°F.
y = 3(90) − 60 = 270 − 60 = 210 cups ✓
Question 7
Data was collected for x = 2 to x = 8. A student uses the equation to predict y at x = 25. Is this interpolation or extrapolation?
x = 25 is outside the data range of 2–8 → extrapolation. Less reliable because we don't know if the trend continues. ✓
Question 8
The line of best fit is y = 5x + 10. Which x-value is an example of INTERPOLATION if data was collected for x = 1 to x = 9?
Interpolation = predicting WITHIN the data range (1–9). Only x = 5 falls inside that range. x = 0, 15, 20 are all extrapolation. ✓

Question 1
What is the joint frequency for Period 1–3 students who prefer Morning?
Joint frequency = interior cell. Period 1-3 × Morning = 28. (The highlighted green cell.) ✓
Question 2
What is the marginal frequency for all students who prefer Evening?
Marginal frequency = column total for Evening = 12 + 22 = 34. Marginal means it's at the margin (edge) of the table. ✓
Question 3
What percent of Period 1–3 students prefer Morning? (Show calculation)
28 out of 40 Period 1-3 students prefer Morning → 28/40 = 0.70 = 70% ✓. Divide by the row total (40), not the grand total.
Question 4
What percent of Period 4–6 students prefer Morning?
18 out of 40 Period 4-6 students → 18/40 = 0.45 = 45% ✓
Question 5
Based on Questions 3 and 4 (70% vs 45%), is there an association between class period and morning preference?
70% vs 45% is a large difference. This suggests an association: earlier-period students lean toward Morning. ✓
Question 6
What is the relative frequency of students who prefer Evening (out of 80 total)?
34 out of 80 total → 34/80 = 0.425 = 42.5% ✓. For overall relative frequency, divide by the grand total (80).
Question 7
How many students both prefer Morning AND are in Period 4–6?
Find the cell for Period 4-6 × Morning = 18 ✓
Question 8
If a student is picked at random, what is the probability they are in Period 1-3 AND prefer Evening?
12 students are Period 1-3 AND prefer Evening. Out of 80 total: 12/80 = 15% ✓

🔍 Common Mistakes

Can you spot what went wrong?

Correlation: points go up-left to down-right
✗ Student: Student says: positive correlation
✓ Fix: WRONG! Downhill = NEGATIVE correlation. Uphill = positive.
💡 Think of it like a hill: going up = positive, going down = negative.

⚡ Key Tips for This Unit

✅ Positive: both increase. Negative: one up, other down. None: random.
✅ Line of best fit: approx equal points above and below.
✅ Outlier: far from the pattern. Can distort the trend line.
✅ Joint RF = cell÷grand total. Conditional RF = cell÷row or column total.
