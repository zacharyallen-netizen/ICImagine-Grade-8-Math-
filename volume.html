📦 Volume of 3D Solids

Cylinders · Cones · Spheres · Composite Solids · NC.8.G.9

Key Formulas
🔑 All volume formulas use π (pi ≈ 3.14). On the EOG you can leave answers in terms of π or use 3.14. Always include cubic units (cm³, in³, ft³, etc.).

🥫 Cylinder

V = πr²h

Base area × height

🍦 Cone

V = ⅓πr²h

One-third of a cylinder!

🏀 Sphere

V = ⁴⁄₃πr³

Only needs the radius!

Relationship: Cylinder, Cone & Sphere
🔑 THE BIG RELATIONSHIP: A cone is exactly ⅓ of a cylinder with the same radius and height. Two cones = ⅔ cylinder. So a cone's volume is always ⅓ of the matching cylinder.
⚠️ SPHERE SHORTCUT: If the sphere fits perfectly inside a cylinder (diameter = height = 2r), the sphere takes up exactly ⅔ of the cylinder's volume. This is why V = ⁴⁄₃πr³.
Worked Examples

🥫 Cylinder: r = 5 cm, h = 10 cm

V = πr²h
V = π(5)²(10)
V = π(25)(10)
V = 250π
V ≈ 785.0 cm³

🍦 Cone: r = 3 in, h = 8 in

V = ⅓πr²h
V = ⅓ · π(3)²(8)
V = ⅓ · π(9)(8)
V = ⅓ · 72π
V = 24π
V ≈ 75.4 in³

🏀 Sphere: r = 6 ft

V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
V = ⁴⁄₃ · π(6)³
V = ⁴⁄₃ · π(216)
V = ⁴⁄₃ · 216π = 288π
V ≈ 904.3 ft³

📦 Composite: Cylinder + Hemisphere (r = 4 m, h = 10 m)

Cylinder: V = π(4)²(10) = 160π
Full sphere: V = ⁴⁄₃π(4)³ = 256π/3
Hemisphere = half: 256π/6 = 128π/3 ≈ 42.67π
Total ≈ 160π + 42.67π = 202.67π ≈ 636.4 m³
Common Mistakes
❌ Forgetting ⅓ for cones — The cone formula has ⅓. Without it, you get the cylinder's volume!
❌ Using diameter instead of radius — If the problem gives diameter, divide by 2 first!
❌ Forgetting to cube for spheres — The sphere uses r³, not r². Square the radius for cylinders/cones, CUBE it for spheres.
❌ Missing units — Volume is always in CUBIC units (cm³, in³, ft³).
📝 Worked Example

Volume of a cone: diameter = 14 cm, height = 9 cm

Step 1: Diameter = 14, so r = 7. h = 9
Step 2: Formula: V = ⅓πr²h
Step 3: Substitute: V = ⅓π(7²)(9) = ⅓π(49)(9)
Step 4: Multiply: V = ⅓(441π)
Step 5: V = 147π ≈ 461.8 cm³ ✓
🔍 Spot the Error: Cylinder: r = 5, h = 10
✗ Student: V = π(5)(10) = 50π ≈ 157.1
✓ Fix: Forgot to SQUARE the radius! V = π(5²)(10) = π(25)(10) = 250π ≈ 785.4
💡 The formula is πr²h — that ² on the radius is critical!
🔍 Spot the Error: Sphere: diameter = 12
✗ Student: r = 12, V = ⁴⁄₃π(12³) = ⁴⁄₃π(1728) ≈ 7238.2
✓ Fix: Used DIAMETER instead of radius! r = 6, not 12. V = ⁴⁄₃π(6³) = ⁴⁄₃π(216) = 288π ≈ 904.8
💡 Always check: did the problem say diameter or radius? Diameter ÷ 2 = radius.
