2 Parallel Lines & Transversals

📌 REMEMBER:
• Corresponding, Alternate Interior, Alternate Exterior, and Vertical angles are CONGRUENT (equal).
• Same-Side Interior (co-interior) and Same-Side Exterior angles are SUPPLEMENTARY (add to 180°).
Two parallel lines (m ∥ n) cut by transversal t form 8 angles. Corresponding: (1,5)(2,6)(3,7)(4,8) · Alt. Interior: (3,6)(4,5) · Alt. Exterior: (1,8)(2,7) · Same-Side Interior: (3,5)(4,6)

3 Triangle Theorems

📌 TRIANGLE ANGLE SUM: The three interior angles of a triangle always add up to ?°.
📌 EXTERIOR ANGLE THEOREM: An exterior angle equals the ? of the two non-adjacent (remote) interior angles.

Problem 1 — Triangle Angle Sum: x + 72 + 55 = 180

Solve: x + 127 = 180 → x = click to reveal 53°

Problem 2 — Exterior Angle Theorem: x + 65 = 140

Solve: x + 65 = 140 → x = click to reveal 75°
ERROR ANALYSIS Jordan's Exterior Angle Mistake — A triangle has interior angles 35° and 70°. Exterior angle = x. Jordan wrote x = 70 − 35 = 35°.
Jordan subtracted instead of adding. The exterior angle = SUM of the remote interior angles: x = 35 + 70 = 105°.

★ Answers

Guided Notes

4.1: Complementary → 90° (a + b = 90°); Supplementary → 180° (a + b = 180°); Vertical = opposite angles when lines cross, always EQUAL; Linear Pair = adjacent angles on a straight line, add to 180°. 4.2: Corresponding/Alt. Interior/Alt. Exterior/Vertical = CONGRUENT (set equal); Same-Side Interior & Exterior = SUPPLEMENTARY (add to 180°). Worked: 3x + 15 = 5x − 7 → x = 11 → angle = 48°. 4.3: Angle Sum = 180°; Exterior = SUM of remote interiors. Problem 1: x = 53°. Problem 2: x = 75°. Error analysis: x = 35 + 70 = 105°.
