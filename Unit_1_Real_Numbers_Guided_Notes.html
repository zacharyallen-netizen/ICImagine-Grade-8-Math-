ERROR ANALYSIS A student claims: "√49 is irrational because it has a square root symbol." What's the mistake?
The square-root symbol does not make a number irrational. √49 = 7, a whole number — so it is rational. A root is irrational only when the number under it is not a perfect square (like √7 or √2).

5 Estimating Square & Cube Roots

📌 THE BIG IDEA: Most square roots are irrational — they don't come out evenly. We can estimate them using two methods!

Method 1: Number Line Placement

Step 1: Find the two consecutive ______ perfect squares your number falls between.
Step 2: Take the ______ square root of each — those are your two consecutive integers.
Step 3: Decide: Is your number closer to the ______ lower or ______ upper perfect square?
Step 4: Place it on the number line ______ accordingly.

Method 2: The 4-Step Algorithm

√n ≈ lower root + (n − lower²) ÷ (upper² − lower²)

Practice: Estimate √45

Step 1: click to reveal 36 < 45 < 49 → √36 = 6, √49 = 7
Step 2 — Fraction: click to reveal (45 − 36) ÷ (49 − 36) = 9 ÷ 13
Step 3: click to reveal 9 ÷ 13 ≈ 0.69
✓ Step 4: click to reveal 6 + 0.69 = 6.69 → √45 ≈ 6.7 (Actual: 6.708)
💡 WHICH METHOD TO USE?
• Number Line: Great for seeing roughly WHERE the root falls — perfect for "between which integers" questions.
• Algorithm: Great for getting a decimal approximation — more precise for "estimate to the nearest tenth."
• Both start with the same Step 1: find the two perfect squares that surround your number!

📌 MIXED NUMBERS: Convert to improper fractions OR decimals. Decimals are usually faster! Mixed number → Decimal: whole number + (numerator ÷ denominator). Example: 3⅖ = 3 + (2 ÷ 5) = 3 + 0.4 = 3.4
⚠️ WATCH OUT:
• When comparing, make sure you're using the same number of decimal places — it's easy to mis-read 3.375 vs 3.5 if you look at them quickly!
• Irrational numbers like √7 need to be estimated first before you can place them in order.
ERROR ANALYSIS To order 1.5, √2, 7/4, √3, a student wrote 1.5 < √2 < √3 < 7/4. Are they right? Explain.
Convert first: 1.5 = 1.5, √2 ≈ 1.41, 7/4 = 1.75, √3 ≈ 1.73. The student forgot that √2 ≈ 1.41 is the smallest. Correct order: √2 < 1.5 < √3 < 7/4.

★ Guided Notes — Answers

1.1 Converting Numbers

3/4 = 0.75 · 1/8 = 0.125 · 1/3 = 0.333… = 0.3̅ · Repeating decimals are RATIONAL; non-repeating, non-terminating decimals are IRRATIONAL. 0.45 → 45% · 75% → 0.75 · 0.75 = 75/100 = 3/4.

1.2 Number Classification

Natural = Counting numbers · Whole = Naturals + zero · Integers = Whole numbers + negatives · Rational = can be written as a fraction · Irrational = non-repeating, non-terminating.

1.3 Perfect Squares & Cubes

3²=9, 4²=16, 5²=25, 8²=64, 9²=81, 10²=100, 13²=169, 14²=196, 15²=225. Cubes: 1³=1, 2³=8, 3³=27, 4³=64, 5³=125, 6³=216, 7³=343, 8³=512, 9³=729, 10³=1000. ∛125 = 5 (inverse of cubing). √20: 16<20<25 → 4 + 4/9 ≈ 4.44 → ≈ 4.4.

1.4 The 9's Rule

1 digit → /9 (0.3̅ = 1/3) · 2 digits → /99 (0.2̅7̅ = 27/99 = 3/11) · 3 digits → /999 (0.1̅2̅5̅ = 125/999).

1.5 Estimating Square & Cube Roots

Square roots — find the two perfect squares around it, then estimate. √45: 36<45<49 → 6 + 9/13 ≈ 6.69 → ≈ 6.7. √30 ≈ 5.5. Cube roots — use perfect cubes: ∛30 (27<30<64) ≈ 3.1; ∛70 (64<70<125) ≈ 4.1. Negative roots flip the sign: −√55 ≈ −7.4.

1.6 Ordering

√5 ≈ 2.24, 2¼ = 2.25, 7/3 ≈ 2.33, 2.1 = 2.1 → 2.1 < √5 < 2¼ < 7/3.
