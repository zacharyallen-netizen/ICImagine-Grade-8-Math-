1.1 Converting Numbers & Repeating Decimals

Fraction → Decimal: DIVIDE numerator by denominator

  • 34 → 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
  • 18 → 1 ÷ 8 = 0.125

Long Division — Showing the Work

3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
1 ÷ 8 = 0.125

Dealing with Repeating Decimals

🔑 REPEATING DECIMALS: When division keeps going with the same pattern, use a BAR over the repeating digits.
💡 KEY INSIGHT: Repeating decimals are RATIONAL because they can be written as fractions! Non-repeating, non-terminating decimals (like π) are IRRATIONAL.

⭐ Converting Repeating Decimals TO Fractions (The 9's Rule)

🔑 THE 9's RULE: The repeating digits go in the NUMERATOR. The DENOMINATOR uses 9's — one 9 for each repeating digit!

• 1 repeating digit → denominator is 9
• 2 repeating digits → denominator is 99
• 3 repeating digits → denominator is 999

Example: Convert 0.27 to a fraction

The digits 27 repeat (2 digits)
Numerator: 27
Denominator: 99 (two 9's for two digits)
2799 = 311 (simplify by dividing by 9)
Answer: 0.27 = 311 ✓

1.3 Perfect Squares, Cubes & Roots

🔑 THE RELATIONSHIP: Squaring and square roots are INVERSE OPERATIONS - they undo each other!

If 5² = 25, then √25 = 5. Know one, know the other!
🔑 MEMORIZE 1-15:
1²=12²=43²=94²=165²=25
6²=367²=498²=649²=8110²=100
11²=12112²=14413²=16914²=19615²=225

Perfect Cubes & Cube Roots

🔑 CUBES & CUBE ROOTS: Cubing and cube roots are INVERSE OPERATIONS too! If 5³ = 125, then ∛125 = 5. The cube-root symbol carries a small 3: ∛.
🔑 MEMORIZE THE PERFECT CUBES 1-10:
1³=12³=83³=274³=645³=125
6³=2167³=3438³=5129³=72910³=1000
💡 √ vs ∛ — DON'T MIX THEM UP: √64 = 8 (what number times itself = 64?) but ∛64 = 4 (what number used three times = 64?). Same radicand, different root — always check for the little 3!

Estimating Cube Roots

🔑 SAME METHOD, PERFECT CUBES: Find the two consecutive perfect cubes your number falls between, then estimate to the nearest tenth — just like square roots, but with cubes.

Example: Estimate ∛30

Step 1: Find the perfect cubes: 27 < 30 < 64 → ∛27 = 3, ∛64 = 4
Step 2: 30 is just barely above 27, so the answer is just above 3
Step 3: ∛30 ≈ 3.1 ✓ (check: 3.1³ ≈ 29.8, very close to 30!)
⚠️ NEGATIVE SQUARE ROOTS: A negative sign in front of a root just flips the estimate to the other side of zero. Estimate the positive root first, then make it negative: −√40 → √40 ≈ 6.3, so −√40 ≈ −6.3.

Estimating Irrational Square Roots

🔑 THE BIG IDEA: Most square roots are irrational — they don't come out evenly. We can estimate them using two methods!

Method 1: Number Line Placement

Step 1: Find the two consecutive perfect squares your number falls between.
Step 2: Take the square root of each — those are your two consecutive integers.
Step 3: Decide: Is your number closer to the lower or upper perfect square?
Step 4: Place it on the number line accordingly.

Method 2: The 4-Step Algorithm

🔑 THE FORMULA: √n ≈ lower root + (n − lower²) ÷ (upper² − lower²)

⚠️ IMPORTANT: The lower root (the whole number part) stays as the FIRST PART of your estimate. You are adding the fraction to the lower root — don't forget to keep it!

Practice: Estimate √45

Step 1: 36 < 45 < 49 → √36 = 6, √49 = 7
Step 2: Fraction: (45 − 36) ÷ (49 − 36) = 913
Step 3: 9 ÷ 13 ≈ 0.69
Step 4: 6 + 0.69 = 6.69 → √45 ≈ 6.7 ✓ (Actual: 6.708)
💡 WHICH METHOD TO USE?
• Number Line: Great for seeing roughly WHERE the root falls — perfect for "between which integers" questions.
• Algorithm: Great for getting a decimal approximation — more precise for "estimate to the nearest tenth."
• Both start with the same Step 1: find the two perfect squares that surround your number!

1.4 Ordering Rational & Irrational Numbers

🔑 THE STRATEGY: To compare and order a mix of fractions, decimals, mixed numbers, and irrational numbers — convert everything to decimals first!

Ordering on a Number Line

Long Division Used Above: 7 ÷ 3

7 ÷ 3 = 2.333...

Ordering Mixed Numbers

🔑 MIXED NUMBERS: Convert to improper fractions OR decimals. Decimals are usually faster!

Mixed number → Decimal: whole number + (numerator ÷ denominator)
Example: 3⅖ = 3 + (2÷5) = 3 + 0.4 = 3.4

Order from greatest to least: 3½, 3⅔, 3⅜, 3.6

Convert all to decimals:
3½ = 3 + (1÷2) = 3.5
3⅔ = 3 + (2÷3) = 3.666... (see long division below)
3⅜ = 3 + (3÷8) = 3.375
3.6 = 3.6
Compare: 3.667 > 3.6 > 3.5 > 3.375
Greatest to Least: 3⅔ > 3.6 > 3½ > 3⅜ ✓
2 ÷ 3 = 0.666... (for 3⅔)
3 ÷ 8 = 0.375 (for 3⅜)

Order from least to greatest: √7, 2⅗, 2.5, 125

Convert all to decimals:
√7 ≈ 2.65 (between √4=2 and √9=3; fraction: (7−4)/(9−4) = 3/5 = 0.6; so 2+0.6 ≈ 2.6)
2⅗ = 2 + (3÷5) = 2.6 (see long division below)
2.5 = 2.5
125 = 12 ÷ 5 = 2.4 (see long division below)
Compare: 2.4 < 2.5 < 2.6 < 2.65
Least to Greatest: 125 < 2.5 < 2⅗ < √7 ✓
3 ÷ 5 = 0.6 (for 2⅗)
12 ÷ 5 = 2.4
⚠️ WATCH OUT:
• When comparing, make sure you're using the same number of decimal places — it's easy to mis-read 3.375 vs 3.5 if you look at them quickly!
• Irrational numbers like √7 need to be estimated first before you can place them in order.
