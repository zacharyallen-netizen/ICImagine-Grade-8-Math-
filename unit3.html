3.6 ⭐ LINEAR INEQUALITIES ⭐

⭐ Equations vs. Inequalities: What's the Difference?

⚠️ THE BIG RULES THAT MAKE INEQUALITIES DIFFERENT:

✓ Rule 1 — Solving: You solve inequalities the same way as equations, except…
⚠️ Rule 2 — FLIP THE SIGN: When you multiply or divide both sides by a NEGATIVE number, you MUST flip the inequality sign!
Example: −2x > 6 → divide by −2 → x < −3 (sign flipped!)

✓ Rule 3 — Graphing: Equations graph as a point; inequalities graph as an arrow on a number line (open circle for strict < >, closed circle for ≤ ≥).

✓ Rule 4 — Infinite Solutions: Unlike equations, inequalities typically have infinitely many solutions (the whole shaded region).
🔑 INEQUALITY SYMBOLS:
• < means "less than" → open circle, shade LEFT
• > means "greater than" → open circle, shade RIGHT
• ≤ means "less than or equal" → CLOSED circle, shade LEFT
• ≥ means "greater than or equal" → CLOSED circle, shade RIGHT
⚠️ CRITICAL RULE - FLIP THE SIGN:

When you MULTIPLY or DIVIDE by a NEGATIVE number, you must FLIP THE INEQUALITY SIGN!

Example (No flip needed): 2x + 3 > 11

2x + 3 > 11
2x > 8 (subtracted 3)
x > 4 ✓ (divided by positive 2, no flip)

Example (FLIP NEEDED): -3x + 4 ≥ 13

-3x + 4 ≥ 13
-3x ≥ 9 (subtracted 4)
x ≤ -3 ✓ (divided by -3, FLIPPED ≥ to ≤)

Multi-Step: 4x - 7 ≤ 2x + 5

4x - 7 ≤ 2x + 5
4x - 2x - 7 ≤ 5 (move 2x to left)
2x - 7 ≤ 5
2x ≤ 12
x ≤ 6 ✓ (divided by positive 2, no flip)

Example Problems (Equations)

Problem 1: Movie Theater

A movie theater charges a $6 entry fee plus $4 per ticket. If the total cost was $30, how many tickets were purchased?
Define: Let x = number of tickets
Equation: 6 + 4x = 30
Solve: 4x = 30 - 6 = 24 → x = 24 ÷ 4 = 6
Answer: 6 tickets were purchased ✓

Problem 2: Phone Bill

A phone plan charges $25 monthly plus $0.10 per text. If the total bill was $37, how many texts were sent?
Define: Let x = number of text messages
Equation: 25 + 0.10x = 37
Solve: 0.10x = 37 - 25 = 12 → x = 12 ÷ 0.10 = 120
Answer: 120 text messages were sent ✓

Example Problems (Inequalities)

Problem 3: Notebook Budget

A student wants to spend no more than $50 on notebooks that cost $6 each. How many notebooks can the student buy?
Define: Let x = number of notebooks
Inequality: 6x ≤ 50 ("no more than" = ≤)
Solve: x ≤ 50 ÷ 6 = 8.33...
Answer: At most 8 notebooks (can't buy partial notebooks!) ✓

Problem 4: Gym Hours

A gym requires members to work out at least 12 hours per month. If a member has already worked out 5 hours, how many more hours must they work out?
Define: Let x = additional hours needed
Inequality: 5 + x ≥ 12 ("at least" = ≥)
Solve: x ≥ 12 - 5 = 7
Answer: At least 7 more hours ✓

Multi-Step Word Problems

⚠️ MULTISTEP CHECK — Before solving:
• Are there parentheses? → DISTRIBUTE first!
• Are there like terms? → COMBINE them!
• Then solve using inverse operations.

Problem 5: Streaming Service (Multi-Step)

A streaming service charges $5 per month plus $3 for each device. A family has 2 identical accounts, each with the same number of devices. If the total monthly cost is $46, how many devices are on each account?
Define: Let x = devices per account
Expression for 1 account: 5 + 3x
Equation: 2(5 + 3x) = 46
Distribute: 10 + 6x = 46
Solve: 6x = 36 → x = 6
Answer: 6 devices per account ✓

Problem 6: School Fundraiser (Multi-Step)

A fundraiser sells boxes of cookies for $4 each and charges a $12 setup fee. The fundraiser is run twice, selling the same number of boxes each time. If the total raised is $88, how many boxes were sold each time?
Define: Let x = boxes sold each time
Cost for 1 fundraiser: 12 + 4x
Equation: 2(12 + 4x) = 88
Distribute: 24 + 8x = 88
Solve: 8x = 64 → x = 8
Answer: 8 boxes were sold each time ✓

📝 Review: Worked Examples & Error Analysis

📝 Solve: 3(2x − 4) = 5x + 2

Step 1: Distribute: 6x − 12 = 5x + 2
Step 2: Subtract 5x: x − 12 = 2
Step 3: Add 12: x = 14
Step 4: Check: 3(2·14 − 4) = 3(24) = 72 and 5(14)+2 = 72 ✓

📝 Solve: −3x + 7 ≤ −8

Step 1: Subtract 7: −3x ≤ −15
Step 2: Divide by −3 → FLIP THE SIGN: x ≥ 5
Step 3: Graph: closed circle at 5, arrow pointing RIGHT ✓

🔍 Spot the Error: Solve: −4x > 20

✗ Student work: −4x > 20 → x > 20 ÷ (−4) → x > −5
✓ Fix: When dividing by a NEGATIVE, FLIP the sign! Correct: x < −5
💡 Dividing or multiplying by a negative ALWAYS flips the inequality sign.

🔍 Spot the Error: Solve: 2(x + 3) = 2x + 5

✗ Student work: 2x + 6 = 2x + 5 → 6 = 5 → No Solution ✓
✓ Fix: This one is actually correct! 6 ≠ 5, so no solution.
💡 When variables cancel and you get a FALSE statement, it means no solution. TRUE statement = infinite solutions.

🔍 Spot the Error: Solve: 5x − 3 = 2x + 9

✗ Student work: 5x − 2x = 9 − 3 → 3x = 6 → x = 2
✓ Fix: Moving −3 to the right: it becomes +3, not −3. Should be 9 + 3 = 12, so 3x = 12, x = 4
💡 When you move a term to the other side, its sign changes! −3 becomes +3.
