📦 Volume Practice

Cylinders · Cones · Spheres · Composite Solids · NC.8.G.9

Score: 0|Answered: 0 / 12
Cylinder: V = πr²h    Cone: V = ⅓πr²h    Sphere: V = ⁴⁄₃πr³

π ≈ 3.14 · Always use cubic units · Remember: radius = diameter ÷ 2

💡 Tip: A cone is exactly ⅓ of a cylinder with the same dimensions. When you see "diameter," divide by 2 to get radius first!
🥫 Cylinder Problems
Problem V1
A cylinder has radius 3 cm and height 10 cm. What is the volume? (Use π ≈ 3.14)
V = π(3)²(10) = π(9)(10) = 90π ≈ 282.6 cm³
Problem V4
A cylinder has diameter 8 m and height 5 m. What is the volume?
Diameter = 8, so r = 4. V = π(4)²(5) = π(16)(5) = 80π ≈ 251.2 m³
Problem V10
A cylindrical water tank (r = 2 ft, h = 6 ft) is half full. How much water is inside?
Full: V = π(2)²(6) = 24π. Half full: 24π ÷ 2 = 12π ft³
🍦 Cone Problems
Problem V2
A cone has radius 4 in and height 9 in. What is the volume?
V = ⅓π(4)²(9) = ⅓π(16)(9) = ⅓(144π) = 48π ≈ 150.72 in³
Problem V5
A cone and cylinder have the same radius and height. The cylinder's volume is 120π cm³. What is the cone's volume?
A cone is ⅓ of a cylinder! 120π ÷ 3 = 40π cm³
Problem V7
A cone has radius 6 cm and height 12 cm. What is the volume? (Use π ≈ 3.14)
V = ⅓π(6)²(12) = ⅓π(36)(12) = ⅓(432π) = 144π ≈ 452.16 cm³
🏀 Sphere Problems
Problem V3
A sphere has radius 3 ft. What is the volume?
V = ⁴⁄₃π(3)³ = ⁴⁄₃π(27) = 36π ≈ 113.1 ft³
Problem V6
A sphere has diameter 10 in. What is the volume?
Diameter = 10, r = 5. V = ⁴⁄₃π(5)³ = ⁴⁄₃π(125) = 500π/3 ≈ 523.3 in³
Problem V8
A basketball has radius 4.7 in. Approximately what is its volume? (Use π ≈ 3.14)
V = ⁴⁄₃π(4.7)³ = ⁴⁄₃(3.14)(103.823) ≈ 434.7 in³
📦 Mixed & Composite
Problem V9
Two identical cones are placed base-to-base. Each cone has r = 3 cm and h = 5 cm. What is the total volume?
One cone: V = ⅓π(3)²(5) = 15π. Two cones: 2(15π) = 30π cm³
Problem V11
Which shape has the greatest volume? r = 5, h = 10 for all.
Cylinder: π(25)(10) = 250π. Cone: ⅓(250π) ≈ 83.3π. Sphere: ⁴⁄₃π(125) ≈ 166.7π. Cylinder wins!
Problem V12
A hemisphere (half sphere) has radius 8 cm. What is its volume?
Full sphere: ⁴⁄₃π(8)³ = 2048π/3. Hemisphere = half: 2048π/6 = 1024π/3 cm³

📝 Worked Examples

Study these step-by-step solutions before attempting the problems above.

Volume of a cone: diameter = 14 cm, height = 9 cm
Step 1: Identify r — diameter = 14, so r = 7
r = 7, h = 9
Step 2: Write the formula
V = ⅓πr²h
Step 3: Substitute
V = ⅓π(7²)(9) = ⅓π(49)(9)
Step 4: Multiply inside
V = ⅓(441π)
Step 5: Divide by 3
V = 147π ≈ 461.8 cm³ ✓

🔍 Common Mistakes — Can You Spot the Error?

Each problem has a mistake. Identify what went wrong.

Cylinder: r = 5, h = 10
✗ Student: V = π(5)(10) = 50π
✓ Fix: Forgot to SQUARE the radius! V = π(5²)(10) = π(25)(10) = 250π
💡 The formula is πr²h — that ² is critical!
Cone: r = 6, h = 10
✗ Student: V = π(6²)(10) = 360π
✓ Fix: That's the CYLINDER formula! Cones need ⅓. V = ⅓π(36)(10) = 120π
💡 A cone is always ⅓ of the matching cylinder.
Sphere: diameter = 12
✗ Student: V = ⁴⁄₃π(12)³ = ⁴⁄₃π(1728) = 2304π
✓ Fix: Used DIAMETER instead of radius! r = 6, not 12. V = ⁴⁄₃π(6)³ = ⁴⁄₃π(216) = 288π
💡 Always check: did the problem give diameter or radius? Diameter ÷ 2 = radius.
