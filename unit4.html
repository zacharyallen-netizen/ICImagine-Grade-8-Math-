Unit 4: Geometry: Angles & Triangles

Angle Relationships · Parallel Lines & Transversals · Triangle Theorems · NC.8.G.5

📋 NC Standards Addressed

  • NC.8.G.5 – Use informal arguments to establish facts about angle sums and exterior angles of triangles, angles created when parallel lines are cut by a transversal, and the angle-angle criterion for similarity of triangles.

4.1 Angle Relationships

🔑 COMPLEMENTARY: Two angles that add to 90°
🔑 SUPPLEMENTARY: Two angles that add to 180°
🔑 VERTICAL ANGLES: Opposite angles when lines cross → ALWAYS EQUAL
🔑 LINEAR PAIR: Adjacent angles on a line → Add to 180°

4.2 Parallel Lines & Transversals

Angle Pair Descriptions & Examples

🔑 CORRESPONDING ANGLES: Angles in the same position at each intersection (both above or both below the line, on the same side of the transversal). Corresponding angles are CONGRUENT (equal).

Example: Solve using Corresponding Angles

Given: ∠1 = 72°. Find ∠5.
∠1 and ∠5 are corresponding angles → they are EQUAL
∠5 = 72° ✓
🔑 ALTERNATE INTERIOR ANGLES: Angles between the parallel lines on opposite sides of the transversal. Alternate interior angles are CONGRUENT (equal).

Example: Solve using Alternate Interior Angles

Given: ∠4 = 110°. Find ∠6.
∠4 and ∠6 are alternate interior angles → they are EQUAL
∠6 = 110° ✓
🔑 ALTERNATE EXTERIOR ANGLES: Angles outside the parallel lines on opposite sides of the transversal. Alternate exterior angles are CONGRUENT (equal).

Example: Solve using Alternate Exterior Angles

Given: ∠2 = 65°. Find ∠8.
∠2 and ∠8 are alternate exterior angles → they are EQUAL
∠8 = 65° ✓
🔑 SAME-SIDE INTERIOR (CO-INTERIOR) ANGLES: Angles between the parallel lines on the same side of the transversal. Same-side interior angles are SUPPLEMENTARY (add to 180°).

Example: Solve using Same-Side Interior Angles

Given: ∠3 = 125°. Find ∠6.
∠3 and ∠6 are same-side interior angles → they add to 180°
∠6 = 180° − 125°
∠6 = 55° ✓

4.3 Triangle Theorems

Interior Angle Sum Theorem

🔑 INTERIOR ANGLE SUM: All 3 angles in ANY triangle = 180°

Example: Find missing angle

Triangle has angles: 65° and 80°. Find third angle.
65 + 80 + x = 180
145 + x = 180
x = 35° ✓

Exterior Angle Theorem

🔑 EXTERIOR ANGLE: An exterior angle equals the SUM of the two REMOTE (non-adjacent) interior angles!

Example: Remote interior angles are 50° and 80°

Exterior angle = 50° + 80° = 130° ✓

Example: Given the exterior angle, find the missing remote interior

The exterior angle is 140°. One remote interior angle is 65°. Find the other remote interior angle.
Exterior angle = sum of remote interior angles
140° = 65° + x
x = 140° − 65°
x = 75° ✓

📝 Review: Worked Examples & Error Analysis

📝 Parallel lines: ∠1 = 3x + 15, ∠2 = 5x − 7 (alternate interior)

Step 1: Alternate interior angles are EQUAL: 3x + 15 = 5x − 7
Step 2: Subtract 3x: 15 = 2x − 7
Step 3: Add 7: 22 = 2x
Step 4: Divide by 2: x = 11
Step 5: Find angle: 3(11) + 15 = 48° ✓

🔍 Spot the Error: Triangle angles: 55°, 70°, and x

✗ Student work: 55 + 70 + x = 180 → 125 + x = 180 → x = 65°
✓ Fix: 180 − 125 = 55, not 65! Arithmetic error. Correct: x = 55°
💡 Double-check your subtraction, especially under time pressure.

🔍 Spot the Error: Parallel lines: corresponding angle 65°, find co-interior x

✗ Student work: Co-interior angles are equal → x = 65°
✓ Fix: Co-interior (same-side interior) angles are SUPPLEMENTARY, not equal. x = 180° − 65° = 115°
💡 Corresponding and alternate angles are EQUAL. Co-interior angles ADD to 180°.
