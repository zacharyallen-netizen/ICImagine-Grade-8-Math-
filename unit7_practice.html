💡 Tip: Positive association = both increase together. Negative = one up, other down. Correlation ≠ causation!
📊 Scatterplots & Correlation (NC.8.SP.1)
Problem 7A
A scatterplot shows that as study hours increase, test scores increase. What type of association is this?
As one variable goes up and the other goes up too, that's a positive association.
Problem 7B
A scatterplot shows points scattered randomly with no pattern. What type of association is this?
No visible pattern = no association between the variables.
Problem 7J
A scatterplot shows that as temperature increases, hot chocolate sales decrease. This is a:
As one goes up and the other goes down → negative association.
Problem 7G
Which correlation is strongest?
The closer |r| is to 1, the stronger the correlation. |0.95| = 0.95 is closest to 1.
📈 Line of Best Fit & Predictions (NC.8.SP.2–3)
Problem 7C
A line of best fit has equation y = 2x + 10. What does the slope (2) represent?
Slope = rate of change. Here, y increases by 2 for every 1 unit increase in x.
Problem 7D
A line of best fit is y = −3x + 50. What does the y-intercept (50) represent?
The y-intercept is the value of y when x = 0 — the starting point.
Problem 7E
Using y = 2x + 10, predict the value of y when x = 15.
y = 2(15) + 10 = 30 + 10 = 40
Problem 7F
Data collected for x values 1–10. Using the line of best fit to predict y at x = 50 is an example of:
Predicting OUTSIDE the data range = extrapolation. It's less reliable!
📋 Two-Way Tables (NC.8.SP.4)
Problem 7H
In a two-way table, 30 out of 80 boys like pizza and 25 out of 70 girls like pizza. Which group has a higher relative frequency of liking pizza?
Boys: 30/80 = 37.5%. Girls: 25/70 ≈ 35.7%. Boys have a slightly higher rate.
Problem 7I
A two-way table shows: Dog owners who exercise = 45, Dog owners who don't = 15, Non-owners who exercise = 20, Non-owners who don't = 40. Is there an association between owning a dog and exercising?
Dog owners: 45/60 = 75% exercise. Non-owners: 20/60 ≈ 33%. Clear association!
Problem 7K
Which is NOT a valid conclusion from a correlation?
Correlation does NOT prove causation! Just because two things are related doesn't mean one causes the other.
Problem 7L
A two-way table joint frequency of 'likes math AND is in 8th grade' is 35 out of 200 total students. What is the relative frequency?
Relative frequency = 35/200 = 0.175 = 17.5%

📝 Worked Examples

Study these step-by-step solutions before attempting the problems above.

Two-way table: 45 boys like pizza out of 120 total
Step 1: Joint relative freq
45 ÷ 120 = 0.375 = 37.5%
Step 2: Conditional: of 60 boys
45 ÷ 60 = 0.75 = 75% of boys like pizza

🔍 Common Mistakes

Can you spot what went wrong?

Correlation: points go up-left to down-right
✗ Student: Student says: positive correlation
✓ Fix: WRONG! Downhill = NEGATIVE correlation. Uphill = positive.
💡 Think of it like a hill: going up = positive, going down = negative.

⚡ Key Tips for This Unit

✅ Positive: both increase. Negative: one up, other down. None: random.
✅ Line of best fit: approx equal points above and below.
✅ Outlier: far from the pattern. Can distort the trend line.
✅ Joint RF = cell÷grand total. Conditional RF = cell÷row or column total.
